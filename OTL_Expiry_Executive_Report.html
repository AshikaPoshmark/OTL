★Executive Summary

🔴 Scale of the problem 82.73% of all US OTLs expire without any buyer response — representing 21.7M missed opportunities in a single week across a platform that sends ~4.5M OTLs per day. Even a 1% lift in first buyer response would add ~45K additional responses daily.
🔴 The reach gap — not a buyer intent problem 4.92M buyers were active on the platform during their specific 24hr offer window but had zero awareness — no push delivered, no offer or listing page view of any kind. These are not disengaged users: 96.4% have bought on Poshmark before and 79.7% have specifically purchased via OTL in the last 4 years.
✅ Buyers are already landing on the listing — the discount is just hidden The listing detail page is the #1 path to the offer decision screen (3.66% overall order rate — highest of any tracked channel). Yet 2,022,479 buyers/week visited the listing page while an active OTL existed for them and left without tapping "View Offer" — because the discount amount is invisible until they click. The offer is there; the price signal is not.
✅ The fatigue vs reach split 78.8% of OTL buyers had only 1–5 concurrent OTLs in their inbox — low noise, pure reach failure. Only 1.4% had 51+ concurrent OTLs where fatigue is a factor. The dominant problem is reach and surface visibility, not buyer overwhelm.
⚠️ Without intervention, listings go unsold Only 0.09% of truly unaware buyers purchased the same listing within 7 days — 99.91% of these listings go unsold. Buyers do not return to pay full price. The OTL discount is the primary conversion mechanism for this segment.
💡 Recommendation — no new notifications needed 1. Show the discount on the Listing Detail Page: Replace the plain "View Offer" button with an offer-aware display — show the discount amount inline (e.g. "$165 (17% off)"). The value is already computed; surfacing it removes one tap and converts intent shoppers in the moment.

2. My Likes Discount Badge: Add a discount chip on listing cards in My Likes — catching buyers before they even open the listing. Design precedent exists (NWT, SOLD OUT badges already in this slot).

→ Estimated opportunity: ~50–100K additional orders per week

1Context & Problem Statement

What is OTL?

  • OTL (Offer to Likers) = seller-initiated discounted offer sent to all buyers who liked a listing
  • Offer expires in 24 hours if the buyer doesn't respond
  • 97% of all daily offers on Poshmark are OTLs (~4.5M OTLs/day)
  • Buyer can: accept, counter-offer, or reject
  • Smart Sell V2: auto-sends OTLs within minutes of a like — 17.3% of all OTLs

The Problem

  • 82.73% expire without any buyer response
  • Only 1.57% convert to orders overall
  • 1% lift in first buyer response = ~45K additional responses/day
  • Root cause was unknown: are buyers actively ignoring offers, or genuinely never seeing them?
  • This analysis answers that question definitively
Key distinction "Saw it and ignored it" requires a different intervention than "never knew it existed." This analysis separates the two — and finds that the dominant problem is awareness, not intent.

4Awareness Breakdown — 21.7M Expired US OTLs

Step 1 — Were buyers active on Poshmark during the 24hr offer window?

The first question is whether buyers were reachable at all. Using dw_user_events_daily, each buyer was checked for any valid active session specifically within their offer's 24hr window — from offer_date to offer_expiry_date (the exact period the OTL was live).

16,315,759
Active during their 24hr offer window — 76.7%
4,947,733
Not active during their 24hr offer window — 23.3%
Key finding: 76.7% of buyers whose OTL expired were active on Poshmark specifically during the 24hr window the offer was live. Inactivity explains only 23.3% of expiries — the dominant problem is active buyers who still did not encounter the offer.

Step 2 — For buyers active during the 24hr window, was the offer surfaced through any known channel?

For each active buyer, we checked all channels through which a buyer could have encountered their OTL during the 24hr window. These are the three current notification flows and the offer detail page as the final confirmation of awareness:

📲 Push Notification

  • Delivered to iOS or Android device
  • Buyer can click → lands on offer detail page
  • Eligibility: last active within 90 days + notifications enabled
  • Tracked via dw_user_push_metrics

📰 News Tab

  • In-app feed showing offer activity
  • Buyer sees the OTL offer card in their feed
  • Can tap → goes to offer detail page
  • Tracked via ashika_view_offer_from_news_tab

📁 My Offers / Active Offers

  • Account → Active Offers → My Offers inbox
  • Buyer explicitly browses their offers list
  • Can tap offer → offer detail page
  • Tracked via ashika_my_offers_views
Offer Detail Page = the final decision screen All three awareness flows above converge at the same destination: the Offer Detail Page — the screen where the buyer sees the discounted price and can Accept, Counter, or Reject. A buyer who viewed this page for a specific offer is definitively confirmed as aware. This was tracked via ashika_offer_details_views (joined on offer_id). Any buyer with none of the above signals during the 24hr window is classified as "Truly Unaware."

Three headline findings from the updated funnel

💡 Recommendation: Surface Offers Where Buyers Already Are

Two low-friction, non-intrusive surfaces — no new notifications, no additional OTL volume

1Show Offer Discount on Listing Detail Page PRIMARY
Current UX
Listing page shows a plain "View Offer" button — buyer must tap it to see the offer price or discount. The discount is hidden behind an extra tap, reducing the chance that an intent shopper will act.
Proposed UX
Surface the offer amount or discount directly on the listing detail page, visible without any click:
  • Show "$165 (17% off)" alongside the listing price, or
  • Add a discount badge next to the "View Offer" button: "View Offer — 17% off"
  • Why it works: 3,351,089 OTLs/week (May 10–16) had the buyer on the listing page — 46.51% of those reached the offer decision screen. Showing the discount immediately removes the friction of the extra tap for the remaining 53.49%.
  • Data signal: 2,022,479 truly unaware buyers visited this listing during the active offer window but did not tap "View Offer" — the discount value was invisible to them
  • Intent shoppers convert: Listing detail page entry has the highest overall order rate of any tracked channel — 3.66% overall, 7.86% at decision screen
  • No new infrastructure needed: The OTL amount is already available when rendering the listing page for an eligible buyer
💰 Implied additional orders: ~33K–56K/week
2My Likes Discount Badge SECONDARY
  • Audience: 371K (24hr) to 893K (7d) buyers browsing their liked items
  • What: Discount chip on listing card in My Likes — "17% off" or "$165 offer" displayed directly on the listing thumbnail
  • Design precedent: NWT and SOLD OUT badges already exist in this card slot — same placement, same pattern
  • Key difference from listing page: Catches buyers before they even open the listing — the discount is visible while they browse their liked items
  • Post-expiry: Show an expired-offer badge to prompt new offer initiation (111K post-expiry clickers/week)
💰 Implied additional orders: ~12.5K/week + 2.6K post-expiry
